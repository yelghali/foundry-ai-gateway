# MNT-PUMP-21 — HPX-450 Cavitation Diagnosis and Correction

## Symptom
Rattling or "gravel" noise, erratic pressure, foamy reservoir, and accelerated
wear of the piston shoes. Cavitation is the most common cause of premature
HPX-450 failure.

## Likely causes
1. Inlet pressure below 0.8 bar absolute (suction restriction).
2. Clogged suction strainer.
3. Fluid viscosity too high on cold start (above VG 68 at temperature).
4. Reservoir fluid level low, drawing air.
5. Loose suction-side fitting admitting air.

## Diagnostic procedure
1. Install a compound gauge at the suction port.
2. Start the power unit and read inlet pressure at operating temperature.
3. Inlet below 0.8 bar absolute confirms suction restriction.
4. Inspect and clean the suction strainer; replace if loaded.
5. Verify fluid grade is ISO VG 32–68 and temperature is above -20 °C.
6. Check reservoir level and top up with filtered fluid to ISO 4406 18/16/13.
7. Pressure-test suction fittings with a soap solution for air ingress.

## Correction
- Replace clogged strainers and undersized suction lines.
- Pre-heat fluid to bring viscosity into range before full load.
- Re-torque or replace suction fittings.

## Verification
After correction, inlet pressure must stay above 0.8 bar absolute across the duty
cycle and the gravel noise must disappear. If cavitation persists, inspect the
swash-plate control per the HPX-450 datasheet and escalate to Fabrikam service.
Replacement seals for water-glycol conversions are covered in SDS-HYD-07.
